About black cats

Science confirms that they have real advantages! Stealth mode: Their black color makes them ideal night hunters. They are almost invisible in the dark. Super-immunity? Scientists are investigating the hypothesis that the melanism gene may be associated with increased resistance to certain diseases. Their DNA can hold the secrets of survival. Masters of camouflage: In a dark room, they sometimes only have a pair of glowing eyes, which adds to their mystical charm.

They can "rust". If a black cat spends a lot of time in the sun, its fur may fade and turn a reddish-brown hue. Eye color. Most black cats have bright yellow or green eyes, which creates a stunning contrast. But kittens are born with blue eyes, which then change color. Carrier breeds. Black color can be found in many breeds: from ordinary yard purrs to Bombay cats (which were bred specifically to resemble mini-panthers), Maine Coons, orientals and sphinxes.

So the black cat is not a harbinger of trouble, but a real talisman with a rich history, superpowers and an abyss of charm. They are the living embodiments of night, grace and mystery. And if one of these embers has chosen you, consider yourself very lucky.

Their magical color is the result of an excess of melanin, a pigment called eumelanin. The dominant gene in their DNA is responsible for this. But the most interesting thing is the phenomenon of melanism.
